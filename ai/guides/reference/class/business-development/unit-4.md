## Systems Thinking In Practice

### Baseline Model (Jason's System)

A diagram illustrating how a single person (Jason) operates across two systems:

**Adobe System:**
- Team
- Cross-team Ownership
- Stakeholders

**BYU System:**
- Students
- Contract
- Dept Head

Jason sits at the intersection of both systems, with outputs flowing to:
- Time
- Energy
- Physical Presence

### Shock Introduction

When shocks are introduced to the system, the dynamics change:

**Adobe System (shocked):**
- Exec Demos
- Increased Visibility
- Travel

These create a **Demand Spike** on Jason.

**BYU System (shocked):**
- Students
- Physical Attendance Required (a **Hard Constraint**)

Jason becomes a **Single Point of Failure**, which:
- **Depletes** Time (decreasing)
- **Depletes** Energy (decreasing)
- **Depletes** Physical Presence (decreasing)

### Updated Model (Resilience Through Partners)

The system is restructured to handle the shocks:

**Adobe System:**
- Exec Demos
- Travel
- Demand spike still flows to Jason

**BYU System:**
- Students
- Contract Requirements
- **Casey** is introduced as a **Partner / Coverage Node**

Casey absorbs the **Potential pull managed** from BYU, handling Physical Presence requirements. Jason retains Time and Energy outputs. The demand from Adobe is managed because the BYU constraint is now covered by a partner.

## Note & Vote Process

A structured decision-making method with six steps:

1. **Ask a Question** -- facilitator poses the question to the group
2. **Silent Work** -- everyone writes ideas individually
3. **Silent Share** -- ideas are posted/shared without discussion
4. **Silent Vote** -- everyone votes on their preferred ideas
5. **Debate** -- discuss the top-voted ideas
6. **Decide** -- make a final decision

---

## Small Add: "We're Wrong If..."

Write: **"We're Wrong If..."**

## Step 6: Cheap Loops

- Cheap tests beat expensive opinions
- Always shippable, always learning
- Keep failure recoverable

### STOP: How could we (cheaply) test this?

- Goal: Learn fast if we're wrong
- No "full build" allowed
- Pick 1-2 tests that:
  - Involve real customers
  - Can run in days, not weeks
  - Generate disconfirming evidence
